A 56 year old woman presents to your office with complaints of blood in her urine for the last one week. She also complains of back pain on the right side and burning urination. She has not been eating well for the last one month and has also lost some weight. You order routine lab tests and a CT scan suspecting cancer. CT scan shows an enlarged right kidney with multiple low density areas and a staghorn calculus. A biopsy of the lesions reveals lipid laden macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells and a few multinucleated cells. What is your diagnosis?
